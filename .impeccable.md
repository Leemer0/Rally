## Design Context

### Users

Outly is for potential nightlife users deciding where to go in Toronto before and during a night out. They want a fast, low-friction read on where social momentum is building, then a simple way to make a plan, check in, and unlock an offer without the experience feeling like a dating app.

### Brand Personality

Bold, social, and spontaneous. The emotional anchor is “Say you met at a bar, not a dating app.” Outly should create confidence and anticipation while keeping the tone private, direct, and socially natural.

### Aesthetic Direction

A dark, cinematic, urban editorial experience with sharp lime accents and strong left-aligned typography. Motion should feel like a glimpse of the night already happening: atmospheric and energetic, never frantic. Avoid generic dating-app tropes, over-polished corporate nightlife imagery, decorative glassmorphism, and excessive neon effects.

### Design Principles

- Make the social promise immediately clear; the tagline and primary action always outrank decoration.
- Use one memorable cinematic hero moment, then keep interaction feedback fast and restrained.
- Preserve privacy and user control in both copy and behavior, including reduced-motion alternatives.
- Favor confident hierarchy, asymmetric composition, and purposeful contrast over card-heavy layouts.
- Keep every demo path repeatable and simulator-safe without making the experience feel like developer tooling.
