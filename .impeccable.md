## Design Context

### Users

Outly is for Toronto adults ages 19–40 deciding where to go for a social night out. They are usually planning in the moment, often with friends, and want to know which venues have real momentum before committing. Their core job is to confidently choose a venue, coordinate arrival, meet people in real life, and access any available venue offer without giving up unnecessary personal data.

### Brand Personality

Energetic, effortless, and socially confident. Outly should feel current and nightlife-aware without becoming exclusive, sleazy, or chaotic. The emotional goal is to replace indecision with anticipation and make a spontaneous night out feel easier.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined after-dark city aesthetic: near-black blue-tinted surfaces, sharp off-white type, electric chartreuse accents, restrained metallic detail, and map/city-grid motifs. The visual language should preserve the existing mobile app's Instrument Sans typography, compact controls, rounded sheets, and Toronto-night framing. It should not look like a generic dating app, a neon cyberpunk product, or a luxury nightclub guest-list site.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with the real-world outcome: help people get off their phones and into the city.
2. Make live social momentum legible at a glance through counts, peak times, and location context.
3. Use chartreuse sparingly for decisions and verified activity, not as ambient decoration.
4. Keep privacy claims precise and grounded in the product flow.
5. Design mobile-first for people making decisions while already out, then expand into an editorial desktop layout.
